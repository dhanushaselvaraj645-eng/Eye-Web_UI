Frequently Asked Questions

Answers to the questions we hear most often from our patients.

How often should I test my eyes?

Adults should have a comprehensive eye exam every 1–2 years, and annually after age 40 or if you have diabetes, high blood pressure, or a family history of eye disease. Children should be screened before school age and then periodically through their school years.

Does LASIK hurt?

No. Numbing eye drops are used before the procedure, so patients typically feel only mild pressure, not pain. Some report slight grittiness or light sensitivity for a day or two afterward, which settles quickly.

Is cataract surgery safe?

Cataract surgery is one of the safest and most commonly performed surgeries worldwide, with a very high success rate. Our femtosecond laser-assisted technique further reduces risk and speeds up recovery time.

What are glaucoma symptoms?

Early-stage glaucoma often has no noticeable symptoms, which is why it's called the "silent thief of sight." As it progresses, it can cause gradual peripheral vision loss, eye pain, and halos around lights. Regular pressure checks are the best way to catch it early.

Can children wear glasses?

Yes, children can safely wear prescription glasses at any age, and early correction is important for proper visual development. Our pediatric team fits comfortable, durable frames designed specifically for young patients.

Do I need a follow-up visit after treatment?

Yes. Follow-up visits let your doctor confirm healing is on track and adjust medication if needed. Your care coordinator will schedule these automatically after your procedure.
