Warning Signs That Need Urgent Care

Sudden Vision Loss

Any sudden dimming, blurring or loss of vision in one or both eyes.

Eye Injury or Trauma

Chemical splashes, sharp object injury, or a strong blow to the eye.

Flashes & Floaters

Sudden flashes of light or a shower of floaters, possible sign of retinal detachment.

Severe Eye Pain

Intense pain, redness and nausea together, possible sign of acute glaucoma.

What to Do

While You're On Your Way

Don't rub the affected eye

Rubbing can worsen injuries, especially for foreign objects or chemical exposure.

Rinse chemical exposure immediately

Flush with clean water for at least 15 minutes before heading to the hospital.

Avoid removing embedded objects

Leave any object in place and cover the eye loosely until you reach us.

Call ahead

Calling our emergency line lets our team prepare for your arrival.

In an eye emergency right now?

Call us immediately — our emergency desk is open 24 hours, every day.
